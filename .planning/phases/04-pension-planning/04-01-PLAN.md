tax_year: 2025

**Sections (numbered to match tax-investment.md pattern):**

**Section 1: Riester-Rente**
- Eligibility: unmittelbar foerderberechtigt (employees paying mandatory GRV, civil servants, ALG I/II recipients, parents in Elternzeit). NOT eligible: most self-employed/freelancers not paying mandatory GRV (exceptions: Handwerker, Lehrer, Hebammen, Kuenstler).
- Mittelbar foerderberechtigt: non-working spouse of an unmittelbar person who is also riesting.
- Zulagen table per D-10: Grundzulage 175 EUR/year, Kinderzulage 300 EUR/child/year (born 2008 or later), 185 EUR/child/year (born before 2008), Berufseinsteigerbonus 200 EUR one-time (under 25).
- Minimum contribution for full Zulagen: 4% of prior-year gross income minus Zulagen received, absolute minimum 60 EUR/year. Formula: `own_contribution = max(0.04 * prior_income - zulagen, 60)`.
- Maximum eligible: 2,100 EUR/year (own contributions + Zulagen together).
- Guenstigerpruefung: Finanzamt auto-applies — whichever is larger (Zulagen or Sonderausgabenabzug benefit) is used. Zulagen are offset against resulting tax benefit.

**Section 2: Ruerup-Rente (Basisrente)**
- Eligibility: any German tax resident. Especially valuable for self-employed not eligible for Riester.
- CRITICAL per research (overriding D-11 which used 2024 values): Hoechstbeitrag 2025 is 29,344 EUR single / 58,688 EUR married (section 10 Abs. 3 S. 1 EStG: 24.7% of BBG West 96,600 EUR). Include a note: "D-11 in planning context referenced 27,566 EUR which was the 2024 value. This doc uses the verified 2025 statutory figure."
- Deductible percentage: 100% since 2023 (vorgezogen from originally planned 2025).
- GRV contributions count toward Hoechstbeitrag — employed users get roughly half the Ruerup space because employer's GRV share fills part of the limit.
- Practical benefit formula: `tax_saving = min(contribution, hoechstbeitrag) * marginal_rate`.
- Advisory note: show tax saving in EUR and net cost after subsidy.

**Section 3: bAV (Betriebliche Altersversorgung)**
- Core mechanism: Entgeltumwandlung — employee redirects gross salary into company pension.
- Tax-free limit 2025 (section 3 Nr. 63 EStG): 8% of BBG West = 8% of 96,600 = 7,728 EUR/year (income tax free). Social-security-free limit: 4% of BBG = 3,864 EUR/year.
- Mandatory employer contribution (section 1a BetrAVG, since 2022): employer MUST add 15% of the employee's redirected amount (for Direktversicherung / Pensionskasse / Pensionsfonds).
- GRV tradeoff: lower gross income means fewer Rentenpunkte per year. Always mention this tradeoff, especially for lower-income employees.
- Advisory framing: bAV is employer-contract-dependent. Guidance must be generic — ask HR about available vehicles and employer contribution levels.
- Entgeltumwandlung tax saving formula: `tax_saving = min(redirect_amount, 7728) * marginal_rate`, `sv_saving = min(redirect_amount, 3864) * 0.207` (approx combined SV rate), `net_cost = redirect_amount - tax_saving - sv_saving`.

**Section 4: Riester vs Ruerup vs bAV Comparison Matrix**
Include the comparison table from RESEARCH.md verbatim (Who it's for, Self-employed eligible, Key benefit, Max annual, Flexibility, Child bonus, Sonderausgabenabzug columns).

**Section 5: Gesetzliche Rente (Statutory Pension) — Projection Constants**
- Rentenwert July 2025: 40.79 EUR/month per Rentenpunkt (+3.74% from 39.32).
- Projection formula: `monthly_statutory_pension = de_rentenpunkte * 40.79`.
- Flag: Rentenwert changes annually on July 1 — requires yearly update.
- Standard retirement age 2025: 66 years 2 months (Wartezeit 5 years).
- Default real return rate: 1.5% per D-04. User-overridable via `pension.expected_real_return_de`.

**Section 6: Cross-Country Projection Constants (DE side)**
- Real return rate: 1.5% (hardcoded default per D-04, overridable per D-05).
- Inflation adjustment formula: `real_accumulated = current_value * (1 + real_rate)^years_to_retirement`.
- D-07 disclaimer text verbatim: "Cross-border pension entitlements require verification by a Brazilian social security lawyer (advogado previdenciario). This projection is an illustrative estimate only — actual benefits depend on contribution history, treaty application, and regulatory changes in both countries."

**Sections:**

**Section 1: PGBL vs VGBL Decision Logic**
- PGBL eligibility (BOTH must be true per D-12): 1) files declaracao completa (modelo completo), not simplificada, 2) has active INSS or statutory pension contribution in the same year.
- PGBL deduction rule: max deductible = 12% of taxable gross annual income. Command reads `countries.brazil.gross_income` from profile. Tax base at withdrawal = FULL amount (principal + gains). Declaration code: Payments/Donations code 36.
- VGBL: use when filing declaracao simplificada, OR already maxed 12% PGBL limit, OR investing purely for gains. Tax base at withdrawal = GAINS ONLY.
- Decision tree: if `ir_regime == "completa"` AND has INSS contribution AND `0.12 * gross_income > 0` then PGBL up to 12% threshold, else VGBL.

**Section 2: Progressive vs Regressive IR Regime**
- Regressive table (per RESEARCH.md, by contribution age per tranche):
  - Up to 2 years: 35%
  - 2-4 years: 30%
  - 4-6 years: 25%
  - 6-8 years: 20%
  - 8-10 years: 15%
  - Above 10 years: 10%
- Note: rate applies PER TRANCHE, not per total balance.
- Progressive table: standard IR salary table at withdrawal (0%, 7.5%, 15%, 22.5%, 27.5%). Effective rate depends on total annual withdrawal amount.
- CRITICAL Law 14.803/24: since 2024, regime choice can be DEFERRED to the moment of withdrawal or benefit receipt. Users no longer need to choose at contract inception. Advisory implication: recommend deferring the decision unless the user has a clear long-horizon commitment (>10 years = regressive almost certainly wins).
- Decision guidance: long horizon (>10 years) + high income -> regressive likely wins (10% vs 27.5%). Short horizon (<4 years) OR low withdrawal income -> progressive may win.

**Section 3: INSS Expat Status (for Cross-Country Projection)**
- Brazil-Germany bilateral agreement in force since 2013.
- Totalization: INSS periods + Deutsche Rentenversicherung periods combined to meet minimum requirements in either system. Each country calculates and pays proportional benefit independently.
- Status enum per D-06: "active" (still contributing), "suspended" (no current contributions, prior contributions remain), "totalization" (intends to use DE-BR agreement).
- DO NOT compute INSS entitlement. Command asks status, explains what each means for the projection, appends D-07 disclaimer.
- Default real return rate: 2.0% per D-04. User-overridable via `pension.expected_real_return_br`.

**Section 4: Cross-Country Projection Constants (BR side)**
- Real return rate: 2.0% (hardcoded default per D-04, overridable per D-05).
- INSS monthly benefit: self-reported by user (not computed).
- Same projection formula as DE side: `real_accumulated = current_value * (1 + real_rate)^years_to_retirement`.
