**Content sections:**

1. **IR Rates by Investment Type** (BRTAX-01):
   Table covering all major asset types with tax rate, exemption, and notes:
   - Stocks (sell gain): 15% normal, 20% day-trade. R$20k/month sell volume exempt for normal operations ONLY (not day-trade). Emphasize day-trade exclusion from exemption.
   - FII (sell gain): 20%, no exemption on capital gains. Dividend treatment is separate (see FII section).
   - FII dividends: exempt under base rule (Law 8,668/1993) — see FII section for Law 15,270/2025 changes.
   - CDB: regressive table — 22.5% (<6 months), 20% (6-12 months), 17.5% (12-24 months), 15% (>24 months). Withheld at source.
   - LCI/LCA: exempt for individual investors.
   - Previdencia PGBL: progressive or regressive regime (chosen at purchase). Contributions deductible up to 12% of gross income. Tax on full withdrawal amount.
   - Previdencia VGBL: regressive table on gains only. Contributions NOT deductible. Better for users who already deduct 12% or use declaracao simplificada.

2. **DARF Calculation and Deadlines** (BRTAX-02):
   - Formula: Monthly gain = Total sales proceeds - Cost basis of sold positions
   - Tax = Monthly gain x rate (15% normal, 20% day-trade)
   - R$20k/month exemption: applies ONLY to normal stock operations (operacoes comuns), NOT day-trade
   - DARF codes: 6015 (normal stock/FII capital gains), 3317 (day-trade capital gains)
   - Deadline: last business day of the calendar month following the gain month (e.g., March gains -> DARF due end of April)
   - Late penalty: 0.33%/day up to 20%, plus Selic interest
   - Payment method: via banking app, Receita Federal website, or SICALC tool
   - Include inline deadline reminder note: "Set a monthly reminder for the last business day of each month to check for unreported gains from the prior month"

3. **Come-Cotas** (BRTAX-03):
   - What: advance tax collected twice yearly on open-end investment funds (fundos de investimento)
   - When: last business day of May and November
   - Rate: minimum rate for fund classification — short-term funds 20%, long-term funds 15%
   - Mechanism: fund redeems shares (cotas) equal to tax owed — investor sees fewer shares, not a cash deduction
   - Scope: applies to Fundos de Renda Fixa, Multimercado, and similar open-end CVM-regulated funds
   - Explicit exclusion: "Come-cotas does NOT apply to: FIIs (Fundos de Investimento Imobiliario), ETFs, or CDBs held directly"
   - Annual reconciliation: at redemption, come-cotas already paid is credited — investor pays the difference only

4. **FII Dividend Exemption** (BRTAX-04, BRTAX-05):
   - Base rule (Law 8,668/1993): FII dividends (rendimentos distribuidos) are exempt from IR for individual investors, provided:
     - FII has at least 50 quotaholders
     - Investor holds 10% or less of total quotas
   - Law 15,270/2025 changes (effective 2026-01-01):
     - Introduces "FII qualificado" (qualified FII) category with different treatment
     - Non-qualified FIIs distributing dividends may face 15% withholding
     - 50-quotaholders / 10%-holding conditions remain for base exemption
   - **Disclaimer block** (per D-12): "The exact interaction between Law 15,270/2025 and the existing exemption for standard listed FIIs has not been fully confirmed by Receita Federal guidance. Verify with a contador before the 2026 tax year. This advisory does not constitute tax advice — see legal disclaimer."
   - Declaration requirements: FII dividends must be declared in IR annual filing even if exempt (Rendimentos Isentos e Nao Tributaveis)

5. **Cross-Border Notes for DE+BR Investors**:
   - Brief note: "If you are also a German tax resident, see the German investment tax section for DBA (Double Taxation Agreement) interaction guidance"
   - Brazilian withholding on investment income for non-residents: 15% standard
   - This section is intentionally brief — the DBA detail lives in the /finyx:tax command output (D-11)

6. **Important Notes**:
   - R$20k/month exemption is for normal stock operations ONLY — day-trade gains are always taxable
   - Come-cotas and FII dividends are different mechanisms — do not confuse them
   - PGBL vs VGBL decision depends on IR regime and 12% income threshold
   - All FII dividends must be declared annually even if exempt from taxation
